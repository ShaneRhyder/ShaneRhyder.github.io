What is a Bird

A bird is a warm-blooded, vertebrate animal from the class Aves, characterized by feathers, beaks, and the ability to lay hard-shelled eggs. Most birds are capable of flight due to their lightweight skeleton and strong wings, although some species, like ostriches and penguins, are flightless and have adapted to running or swimming instead. Feathers play a vital role in insulation, flight, and display, while their beaks are specialized for various diets, such as seeds, insects, or fish, since birds lack teeth. Birds also have high metabolic rates to support their active lifestyles, and many species migrate seasonally. With around 10,000 species, birds are incredibly diverse, inhabiting nearly every environment on Earth, from tropical forests to polar regions.
